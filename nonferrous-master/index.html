  • 04/01/17

    January

    India

    India is poised to overtake South Korea this year as the world’s fifth-largest car manufacturer behind Germany, the USA, Japan and China. Considering that the Indian car industry opened up to liberalisation and globalisation only in the mid 1990s, this is quite an achievement. Today, the automotive industry contributes around 7.5% to India’s GDP. However, with penetration levels still very low at around 30 cars for every 1000 people, it certainly still has plenty of scope for further growth.

    Dhawal Shah, Metco Marketing PVT Ltd,Senior Vice-President of the BIR Non-Ferrous Metals Division

  • 12/02/17

    February

    Mexico

    The Mexican economy is continuing to offer mixed signals. On the one hand, latest figures show domestic vehicle production at record levels; however, despite a 6% increase in shipments to the USA, vehicle exports contracted by 1.5% overall owing to lower shipments to Brazil and other Latin American countries. Mexican smelters related to the automotive industry seem to be experiencing lower demand, apparently caused by a deceleration in US vehicle sales. Lower sales for the smelters and cheap, available primary material are damaging the demand for aluminium scrap in Mexico. What used to be a seller’s market with continuous, robust demand is no longer the same; there are some sales opportunities here and there, but these are not as consistent as before.

    Alejandro Jaramillo, Glorem SC,General Delegate of the BIR Non-Ferrous Metals Division

  • 22/03/17

    March

    Australia

    Market conditions remain challenging throughout Australasia for metal merchants and the wider economies. Material is still relatively slow in coming into yards, and demand is mixed. Metal merchants generally appear to be finding conditions tough; local consumers are buying at current levels and there is demand from traditional offshore markets, but the tone is more subdued for certain grades. There has been further consolidation since our previous report - an illustration of the challenges faced by many merchants and consumers.

    Paul Coyte, Hayes Metals, New Zealand, Vice-President of the BIR Non-Ferrous Metals Division
